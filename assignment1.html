Prof John F Nash Jr

A mathematical genius.

A brief timeline in the life of Prof John F Nash Jr

  • 1950 - Nash earned a PhD with a 28-page dissertation on non-cooperative games
  • 1978 - Nash was awarded the John von Neumann Theory Prize for his discovery of non-cooperative equilibria, now called Nash Equilibria.
  • 1994 - He received the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences (along with John Harsanyi and Reinhard Selten) as a result of his game theory work as a Princeton graduate student.
  • 1999 - Leroy P Steele Prize.
  • 2002 - Class of Fellows of the Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences.
  • 2010 - Double Helix Medal.
  • 2015 - Abel Prize.

Nash's few papers helped lay the foundations of the discipline. Unfortunately, Nash's career came to an end by the time he was 29 years old because of schizophrenia.
